## How This Works (The Boring But Important Part)

### Data Collection: Multi-Agent AI Research
We used **Perplexity AI's Deep Research mode** to crawl thousands of sources across:
- Major news (NPR, Washington Post, NYTimes, BBC, CNN, Al Jazeera)
- Fact-checkers (PolitiFact, Snopes, FactCheck.org)
- Government records (DOJ indictments, Congressional testimony, FDA warnings)
- Social media archives (Trump Twitter Archive, Truth Social)
- Academic research (behavioral studies, Project 2025 docs)

Each incident was cross-verified, timestamped, and scored by AI agents with human oversight on edge cases.

### Entry Selection: How Things Make the Cut
Not every Trump tweet makes the list. To get in, an entry must pass:

| Criteria | Threshold |
|----------|-----------|
| **Verifiability** | â‰¥2 independent sources or 1 primary doc |
| **Significance** | Measurable public/institutional impact |
| **Absurdity/Danger** | Breaks norms, endangers public, or corrodes systems |
| **Pattern Relevance** | Links to existing behavioral clusters |

We reject trivia and unverified gossip. Only the **high-grade crazy** makes it through.

### Storage & Search: PostgreSQL + Vector Embeddings
All 377+ entries live in **Neon PostgreSQL** with:
- Relational tables (entries, sources, keywords, reactions)
- **pgvector extension** for semantic search (find "election interference" even if those exact words aren't used)
- Hybrid search (keywords + meaning) for precision

You can filter by year, category, severity threshold, or ask natural language questions like "show me grift escalation from 2016-2025."
